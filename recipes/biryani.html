Hyderabadi Biryani

Description

Hyderabadi Biryani is a world-famous, aromatic, and flavorful rice dish from Hyderabad, India. It's made with basmati rice, meat (or vegetables), and a blend of rich spices. The "dum pukht" method of slow cooking allows the ingredients to infuse their flavors perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 500g Basmati Rice
  • 500g Chicken or Mutton
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 4 Onions, thinly sliced and fried (Birista)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 cup Mint Leaves
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves
  • 4 Green Chilies
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • 2 tbsp Ghee
  • Saffron strands soaked in milk

Steps

  1. Marinate the meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, half the fried onions, mint, coriander, and powdered spices for at least 4 hours.
  2. Par-boil the basmati rice until it is 70% cooked. Drain the water.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the marinated meat at the bottom.
  4. Add a layer of the 70% cooked rice over the meat.
  5. Sprinkle the remaining fried onions, mint, coriander, saffron milk, and ghee on top.
  6. Seal the pot with a tight lid (traditionally with dough) and cook on low heat (dum) for 25-30 minutes.
  7. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening. Gently fluff the rice and serve hot with raita.
