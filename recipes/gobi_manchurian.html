Gobi Manchurian

Description

Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese appetizer made with crispy fried cauliflower florets tossed in a sweet, sour, and savory Manchurian sauce. It can be served dry as an appetizer or with gravy as a main course with rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium Cauliflower (Gobi), cut into florets
  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 2 tbsp Corn Flour
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 Bell Pepper, diced
  • 2 Spring Onions, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves Garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Tomato Ketchup
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Create a batter using all-purpose flour, corn flour, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and water.
  2. Dip cauliflower florets in the batter and deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Set aside.
  3. In a pan, heat some oil. Saut√© the chopped garlic, bell pepper, and spring onions.
  4. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, and a little water to make the sauce.
  5. Add the fried gobi florets to the pan and toss well to coat them with the sauce.
  6. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve immediately.
